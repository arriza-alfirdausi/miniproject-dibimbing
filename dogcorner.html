Dalmatian

Underneath those spots is a lean, muscular build. Dalmatians are on the larger end of medium-sized dog breeds, standing at 48.3 to 61 centimeters and weighing around 20.4 and 31.7 kilograms when fully grown.

|credit: Lee Hao Chih / getty

German Shepherd

The German Sheperd have an alert-yet-kind expression marked by their perked-up ears and dark, almond-shaped eyes. Weighing around 22.7 to 40.8 kilograms, these large dogs have an unmistakable presence.

|credit: Legolin / getty

Pharaoh Hound

Pharaoh hounds are graceful, elegant dogs with sleek, shiny coats. At around 20 to 25 kilograms and 53 to 63.5 centimeters tall, they are adaptable medium-sized dogs as long as they can burn off some steam with daily exercise.

|credit: Kate / adobe stock

Shiba Inu

Fox-like in appearance, the Shiba Inu's classic color is orangey-red with variations of black and tan, cream, and sesame (black-tipped hairs on a red background). Male Shiba Inu tend to be bigger than their female counterparts.

|credit: Terje Haheim / getty

Siberian Husky

Built for speed and endurance, a Siberian Husky dog is compact, standing about 58 centimeters high and weighing between 15.9 and 27 kilograms. Their coats feature various tones and combination of red, gray, black, and snow white.

|credit: Belyaaa / adobe stock

American Pit Bull Terrier

Commonly known as "pitties", these canines come in many coat colors and patterns like blue, red, black, white, fawn to name a few. They can also vary dramatically in size, from 15.8 to 31.7 kilograms.

|credit: Evdoha / adobe stock

Basenji

The Basenji is a primitive hunting dog breed in a lean—yet strong—and compact size. Their short coat is fine in texture and comes in four main colorways: chestnut red, black, tricolor (black, tan, and white), or brindle (black stripes on a chestnut base).

|credit: Photographs by Maria Itina / getty

Beagle

Easily recognizable by their big brown eyes and square floppy ears, the beagles are small but mighty. There are two types of beagles: A medium-size version (9 to 15.9 kilograms) and a smaller version (less than 9 kilograms).

|credit: Lenyfoto Pet Photography / getty

Great Dane

Nicknamed the "gentle giants", a Great Dane can grow to be the size of a small horse. They can have height spans 71 to 81.3 centimeters at the shoulder, and their weight can be anywhere between 49.9 and 79.4 kilograms.

|credit: Bigandt_Photography / getty

Greyhound

Greyhound will happily lounge around the house or be a companion on a leisure walk, hence the nickname "65-kph-couch potatoes". These large hounds weigh between 27 and 31.7 kilograms, but carry almost no body fat for aerodynamic build.

|credit: Westend61 / getty
